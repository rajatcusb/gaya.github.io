About Gaya

Gaya is a city in the state of Bihar, India. It is a popular tourist destination known for its historical and cultural significance.

Gaya is the second largest city of Bihar. It is 100 kilometers south of Patna, the capital of Bihar. Gaya is situated on the banks of Falgu River (Niranjana). Gaya has historical importance and is one of the major pilgrimage sites of Bihar. This city is especially famous for Hindu pilgrims. The most attractive place of Gaya is Vishnupad Temple. This temple is situated on the banks of Falgu river. According to the Puranas, this temple has been built on the footprints of Lord Vishnu. People believe that Lord Vishnu killed Gayasur by placing his foot on Gayasur's chest. The area of ​​Gaya is 4,976 square kilometers. Its population is 43,91,418. Literacy rate is 52.38%. Gaya has 24 blocks, 2,886 villages, and 4 municipalities.
